## How the team was formed?
The team EatChiwi was formed in an unexpected way, starting with how the members came together. Efraín J. Ortiz Mulero was already participating in the Future Engineer category and had a solid amount of experience in both mechanics and programming. He is also the brother of José F. Ortiz Mulero, who later joined the Future Engineer category. José had previously participated in Robot Mission, where he served as both the mechanic and programmer for his team. He was very dedicated to his work and constantly sought growth, which led him to join Future Engineer. Being teamed up with someone he already knew and lived with made this an even better opportunity. Faneshka Cartagena Maldonado was participating in Robot Sports, where she worked as an engineer, programmer, and note taker for her team. At one point, one of her teammates left their all girls group, leaving her with just one remaining teammate, who eventually left as well. This situation led her to explore new opportunities, and she decided to join the Future Engineer category. Since the team only needed one more member to be complete, she became the perfect addition. The three of them already knew each other well, which helped everything come together naturally. As for the name “EatChiwi,” it comes from Faneshka’s guinea pig, named Chiwi. Efraín thought it would be funny to name the team EatChiwi, since they often joked about how guinea pigs are eaten in some cultures. The team roles were assigned based on each member’s strengths. Efraín took on the role of mechanic, as he excelled with the 3D printer and designs. Faneshka became responsible for documentation because of her strong note taking skills. José focused on programming, as he worked best with coding and learned quickly.
